## 1. The Nature of Thought Lines

Thought Lines are not simple metadata tags. They are thematic vectors that group related Claims and Source Fragments to track the evolution of a concept over time. Seeding a Thought Line creates a narrative container for knowledge without forcing premature synthesis.

## 2. Seeding Prerequisites

Thought Line seeding is allowed only when sufficient Claims or Source Fragments exist to demonstrate a recurring theme or evolving concept. Seeding a Thought Line for a single, isolated Claim is generally discouraged unless it represents a major architectural pivot.

## 3. Grouping Without Synthesizing

When linking Claims to a Thought Line, the goal is aggregation, not resolution. Contradictory Claims and superseded Claims must be included in the Thought Line to preserve the complete historical context. Resolving contradictions is the job of a Decision Thread or a Current Best Synthesis, not the Thought Line itself.

## 4. Avoiding Tag Sprawl

To prevent Thought Lines from degrading into a disorganized tagging system, new Thought Lines should only be created when an existing one cannot adequately contain the concept. Before seeding a new Thought Line, always review `THOUGHT-LINE-REGISTRY.md` to ensure a similar vector does not already exist.

## 5. Review Requirements

Thought Lines require periodic review to ensure they remain focused. If a Thought Line becomes too broad (e.g., "General Architecture"), it should be split into more specific vectors (e.g., "Repository Architecture", "Memory Pipeline Architecture"). This splitting process requires Guardian Architect approval.
